Reference

This calculator evaluates system specific fan power (SFP) from either known fan electrical input power or an estimated power derived from airflow, pressure and overall efficiency.

Key relationships used:

System SFP = Total Fan Input Power ÷ Airflow

Estimated Fan Power = (Flow × Total Pressure) ÷ Overall Efficiency

CIBSE describes SFP as a standardised measure of how much total power is required by the fan to move air through the connected system, commonly expressed as W/(L·s). :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

CIBSE also notes that BS EN 16798-3 provides the framework for determining SFP for non-residential ventilation systems. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

Regulatory note: Approved Document L for England uses maximum SFP values for air distribution systems and states that SFP should be calculated in accordance with BS EN 16798-3 at full design load. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

Scope note: this calculator is an indicative system-design and checking tool. Final compliance should be based on the actual installed system arrangement, design operating point, controls, component losses and the applicable project compliance route.
