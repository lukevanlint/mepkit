Method

Pipe size is selected by comparing the design flow rate against the internal area of each listed pipe size.

Velocity = Flow ÷ Pipe Area

Where heat load is used as the input, flow rate is first calculated from:

Flow Rate (L/s) = Heat Load (kW) ÷ (4.18 × ΔT)
  • Flow Rate = design water flow (L/s)
  • Velocity = water velocity in the selected pipe (m/s)
  • The smallest listed pipe size with velocity at or below the target is selected.

Reference

  • This calculator applies standard LTHW sizing logic by determining design flow rate and comparing it against listed pipe sizes.
  • Copper and steel pipe systems use different size series, so the recommended size depends on the selected material.
  • Velocity guidance shown in the result reflects typical accepted design practice for closed water systems rather than fixed absolute limits.
  • For known flow scenarios, the calculator can be used directly from a previously established duty or schedule flow rate.
Design note: This calculator sizes pipework based on velocity only. Final pipe selection should also consider pressure drop, pump head, noise, erosion risk, fittings, valves, insulation allowance, manufacturer data and project-specific design criteria.
