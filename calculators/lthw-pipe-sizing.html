Method

First, the LTHW flow rate is calculated using the standard heating water relationship:

Flow Rate (L/s) = Heat Load (kW) ÷ (4.18 × ΔT)

The flow is then checked against the internal area of each pipe size:

Velocity = Flow ÷ Pipe Area
  • Heat Load = heating duty (kW)
  • ΔT = design temperature difference (°C)
  • Velocity = water velocity in the selected pipe (m/s)

Reference

  • This calculator uses the standard heating water relationship: Flow Rate (L/s) = Heat Load ÷ (4.18 × ΔT).
  • The calculated flow rate is then compared against standard pipe sizes to identify the smallest pipe that maintains velocity within the selected limit.
  • Copper and steel pipe systems use different standard size series, so the recommended size depends on the selected material.
  • Velocity guidance shown in the result reflects typical accepted design practice for closed water systems rather than fixed absolute limits.
Design note: This calculator sizes pipework based on velocity only. Final pipe selection should also consider pressure drop, pump head, noise, erosion risk, fittings, valves, insulation allowance, manufacturer data and project-specific design criteria.
