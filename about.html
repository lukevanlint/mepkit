About MEPkit

A practical toolkit of engineering calculators for UK building services design.

Overview

MEPkit is a lightweight, web-based toolkit developed to support day-to-day calculations in mechanical, electrical and public health engineering.

The aim is simple — provide fast, clear and reliable tools that can be used during real design work, without relying on complex spreadsheets or fragmented references.

Why MEPkit Exists

Many existing engineering tools are either overly complex, outdated, or difficult to use in a fast-paced design environment.

In practice, engineers often rely on a mixture of spreadsheets, hand calculations and guidance documents to complete routine tasks.

MEPkit was created to provide a simpler alternative — bringing commonly used calculations into a consistent, accessible format.

Methodology

All calculations within MEPkit are based on standard engineering principles and reflect typical UK building services design approaches.

Where applicable, methods align with guidance from sources such as:

  • CIBSE Guides
  • British Standards (BS)
  • Common industry design practices

Calculations are intentionally simplified to support early-stage design, feasibility checks and quick verification of assumptions.

Limitations

The tools provided are intended for indicative use only and do not account for all project-specific conditions, constraints or regulatory requirements.

Outputs should be reviewed and verified as part of a wider design process and should not be relied upon in isolation for final design decisions.

About the Author

MEPkit has been developed by a UK-based building services engineer with experience in real project delivery and practical design workflows.

The toolkit is being built to reflect the kinds of calculations, checks and engineering tasks that regularly come up in day-to-day design work.
